Invited Talk

Jiaying Kou

Australian Urban Research Infrastructure Network (AURIN), Australia


Title: AURIN—Enable, Empower and Encourage Research on Diversity, Equity and Inclusion


Abstract: Australia faces huge challenges in climate change, energy transition, and changing demographics, from the heat island effect to devastating floods and bushfires, from an ageing population to affordable housing. These challenges will increasingly threaten population well-being and safety, infrastructure resilience and sustainable housing, especially with our diversified population, new migrants, and regional population with fewer infrastructure resources.

AURIN provides crucial data and digital infrastructure to support evidence-based urban and regional research and policy development. In this talk, you will discover AURIN's open data and infrastructure to accelerate research and science discovery. Learn about research projects AURIN supports and collaborates with in creating and making accessible hard-to-get data and hard-to-do analysis. These research infrastructure assets will enable, empower, and encourage academia, government and industry to tackle these challenges with the heart of getting the voice of diversity, equity and inclusion.


Bio: Dr Jiaying Kou is a Training Support Officer at AURIN. Her work focuses on developing urban informatics training materials and supporting researchers from different domains. She has been involved in a few high impact research projects at AURIN and established international collaboration. She recently completed her PhD in Computer Science at Victoria University. Her research crosses the disciplines of economics and computer science, applying data science methods to understand housing market economics and location based social networks.
